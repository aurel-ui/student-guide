JUMP TO A SECTION

The Short Answer
Open two accounts.
Not one.
The strategy

Open N26 or Revolut first — you can do it today, from anywhere, in under 10 minutes. This gives you a French IBAN immediately for rent, CAF, and tuition payments. Then open Wise to receive money from your family abroad without losing money on exchange rates. Together these two cover everything a student in Paris needs.

Open alongside
Wise
// Receiving money from abroad

Not a bank — a multi-currency account. When your parents send you money from India, Brazil, the US, or anywhere else, Wise charges the mid-market exchange rate with no hidden markup. Dramatically cheaper than a bank transfer.

  • French IBAN available

If you need a real French bank
BNP Paribas
// Traditional French bank

The only major French traditional bank with a full English interface. Better for long stays, if you need a chequebook, or if your landlord specifically requires a French bank account.

  • Full French IBAN

La Banque Postale is useful if you have no fixed address yet — they are more flexible about accepting students without a permanent lease. Branches are everywhere in Paris. Good fallback if N26/Revolut are rejected during your visa application for requiring a physical French bank statement.

Order of Operations
Do it in this order.

The sequence matters. Opening the wrong account first can cause delays with your visa, your lease, and your CAF application. Follow this order.

01
Before arriving in France — do this now
Open N26 or Revolut

Download the app, verify your identity with your passport, and get your IBAN. Takes 10 minutes. You need this IBAN to prove you can receive your CAF housing aid and to set up SEPA payment for your tuition installments. Do not wait until you arrive in Paris.

What you need to open N26 or Revolut
  • Valid passport or national ID
  • Email address
  • A phone number (any country)
  • A selfie taken in-app for identity verification
  • No proof of address required for N26/Revolut
02
Before or just after arriving
Open Wise for receiving money from abroad

If your family will be sending you money from outside France, open Wise now. Share your Wise IBAN/account details with your parents or sponsor. Every time they send money, it converts at the real exchange rate. Over a full year of monthly transfers, this saves significantly compared to a standard bank wire.

Wise vs bank transfer — a real example
  • Sending €1,000 from India via a standard bank: you might receive €940–960 after fees and poor exchange rates
  • Sending €1,000 from India via Wise: you receive ~€994 after a small transparent fee
  • Over 10 months of transfers, the difference is €300–600 in your pocket
03
Once you have a signed lease
Apply for CAF housing aid (APL)

Go to caf.fr. You need your French IBAN (N26, Revolut, or Wise all work), your lease, and your VLS-TS validation confirmation if you are a non-EU student. Processing takes 2 to 3 months — you will be back-paid for the waiting period, but do not count on this money for your first months.

What CAF needs from you
  • Signed lease (bail meublé or bail de colocation)
  • French IBAN
  • Proof of enrollment at ConceptCore (Certificate of Enrollment)
  • VLS-TS validation confirmation (non-EU students)
  • Passport / ID
  • Your French address
04
If your landlord or lease agency requires it
Open BNP Paribas (if needed)

Some traditional landlords and agencies in France prefer or require a French bank account (IBAN starting with FR). In this case, visit a BNP Paribas branch with your passport, enrollment certificate, and proof of address. Bring your Garantme or Visale certificate too. Open the student account (Compte Étudiant) which has lower fees.

05
Ongoing — set up once, forget about it
Set up SEPA direct debit for tuition

ConceptCore uses GoCardless (via Pennylane) for EU students on a payment plan. Your first mandate activation takes 5 to 6 business days — set this up well before your first installment date (September 5). Use the IBAN from N26, Revolut, or any EU bank account.

Your Setup Checklist
Track your progress.
  • N26 or Revolut account opened. IBAN saved and accessible.
  • Wise account opened (especially if receiving money from outside the Eurozone).
  • Family / sponsor has your Wise account details for sending monthly support.
  • IBAN shared with ConceptCore for SEPA direct debit setup. Done at least 7 days before first payment date.
  • CAF application submitted once lease is signed. caf.fr.
  • Civil liability insurance paid (~€30–60/year). Required before Day 1 at ConceptCore.

Receiving Money from Abroad
How to get money
from your family.
Always use Wise

Tell your family to use Wise (wise.com) to send you money. They send from their local bank account, it arrives in your Wise account, and you move it to N26 for spending. The mid-market exchange rate means you lose almost nothing on conversion. Standard bank wires can cost 3 to 8% in hidden exchange fees — on €1,000/month that is €360–960 lost per year for no reason.

How to receive money — step by step for your family
  • Your family creates a free Wise account at wise.com
  • They enter your Wise IBAN as the recipient
  • They send in their local currency (USD, INR, BRL, etc.)
  • You receive euros in your Wise account, usually within minutes
  • Transfer to N26 for daily spending via free SEPA transfer
For non-EU visa applicants

Wise can generate a European IBAN for you before you arrive in France. This is the IBAN recommended by ConceptCore in the visa guide for proving financial resources — the consulate sees a real account balance, not a promise. See the visa guide for full details on financial proof requirements.

CAF / APL reduces your rent

Once your CAF application is processed (2 to 3 months), you receive monthly housing aid credited directly to your account. Typical amount is €50 to €200/month depending on your rent and situation. It is back-paid — so your first payment covers the months you waited. Do not budget with this money for months 1 and 2.

Student discounts — use them

Your ConceptCore student card unlocks significant savings in Paris. Free entry to all French national museums for under 26 (Louvre, Pompidou, Orsay — free, all year). Half-price cinema tickets. Student rates at most theatres and exhibitions. Reduced rate at university cafeterias (Crous) near the campus. These add up to €50–100/month in savings if you use them actively.

Working While Studying
The 964-hour rule.
For EU / EEA / Swiss students

No restrictions. You can work as much as you want, in any capacity — salaried or freelance. The 964-hour rule does not apply to you. You can register as an auto-entrepreneur (micro-entrepreneur) to take commissions immediately.

For non-EU students on a VLS-TS

You are authorized to work up to 964 hours per year in salaried employment. This is roughly 18.5 hours per week, or 60% of a full-time annual schedule. The counter starts from your VLS-TS validation date and resets when your permit is renewed.

Non-EU students cannot freelance on a student visa

This is a common misunderstanding. Non-EU students on a VLS-TS cannot register as a micro-entrepreneur (auto-entrepreneur) or take freelance commissions while on a student permit. This applies even if the work is done remotely for a foreign client and paid into a foreign account. Violating this risks non-renewal of your residence permit.

If you want to take commissions during your studies, you need to either complete your studies first and transition to a work permit, or seek specific legal advice. The ConceptCore Aftercare program and graduation show are designed to help you do this properly after graduation.

Mandatory internships are protected

If ConceptCore requires an internship as part of your program, the hours worked during that internship do not count toward your 964-hour limit. The internship must be covered by a signed three-party agreement (convention de stage) between you, ConceptCore, and the host studio. Internships over 2 months must be paid a minimum of ~€650/month. This income is tax-exempt up to €20,815/year.

Tax in France
Yes, you need to file.

Everyone who lives in France must file an income tax declaration each spring — even if you had zero income. This is important: a clean tax declaration history makes everything easier when renewing your residence permit and applying for long-term status later.

01
Spring (April–June) every year
File your tax declaration online

Go to impots.gouv.fr. Create an account with your French address and passport. If you had no income, file a zero-income declaration. If you worked, declare your gross earnings. First-time filers receive a paper letter with a numéro fiscal (tax ID) — keep this safe.

02
What counts as income
Know what to declare
Income types
  • Salaried wages (salaire) — declared automatically by your employer via your payslip. You confirm the amount.
  • Mandatory internship income up to €20,815 — tax exempt, but still declare it in the exemption section
  • Scholarships or grants — declare as exempt income
  • Money sent from family — not income, not declared
  • CAF/APL housing aid — not income, not declared
03
After filing
Save your avis d'imposition

After your declaration is processed, you receive an avis d'imposition (tax assessment notice). Save this document — landlords, banks, and the préfecture will ask for it when you renew your residence permit. Download it from impots.gouv.fr and keep a PDF.

The minimum wage in France (SMIC) — 2025

As of January 2025, the gross minimum wage is €11.65/hour. After social security deductions (~20%), you take home approximately €9.22/hour net. A student working 10 hours/week at minimum wage earns about €92 net/week. Working the full 964 hours at minimum wage earns approximately €8,884 net for the year.
