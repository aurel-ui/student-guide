You don't have to live in Arcueil.

Transit Pass
The Navigo pass.
Your options
  • Navigo Mois (Monthly) — unlimited travel on all RER, Metro, Bus, Tram in Île-de-France. ~€86.40/month. Rechargeable on the RATP app or at any station.
  • Navigo Année (Annual) — same unlimited access paid yearly. ~€945/year (~€78.75/month). Best value if you are here for the full academic year.
  • Imagine R (Student Annual Pass) — heavily discounted annual pass for students under 26 enrolled in a French institution. ~€350/year. Requires your ConceptCore student card and proof of enrollment. Apply at any RER/Metro station.
  • Navigo Liberté+ (Pay-as-you-go) — best if you attend hybrid and are not in Paris every week. You are billed per trip at a capped daily rate.
The Imagine R is worth it

If you are under 26 and attending on-site full-time, the Imagine R cuts your annual transport cost by more than half. Apply as soon as you have your student card. It covers all zones — you can take the RER B from campus all the way to CDG Airport at no extra cost.

The Guarantor Problem
Every landlord
will ask for one.
What is a garant?

A garant (guarantor) is a person or institution that legally agrees to pay your rent if you cannot. French landlords require this for almost every rental — it is not optional. Traditionally this must be a French resident earning at least 3× the monthly rent. As an international student without a French guarantor, you have two good alternatives.

Option 01 — Recommended
Garantme
Why this is the best option for internationals

Garantme is a private certified guarantor service that landlords and student residences across France trust and recognize. It is the most widely accepted option for international students. You can apply before arriving in France, directly from your home country, entirely in English.

01
Before you start apartment hunting
Apply online at garantme.fr

Create an account, fill in your profile, and submit your documents. Eligibility is confirmed within minutes. Your Garantme certificate is issued within 24 hours of approval.

Documents needed
  • Your passport or national ID
  • Your ConceptCore Certificate of Enrollment (or pre-enrollment letter)
  • Your sponsor/guarantor's last 3 bank statements and 3 payslips
  • Proof of address of your sponsor
02
Once approved
Receive your certificate

You get a Garantme certificate to include with every rental application. Landlords who see it know immediately that your rent is guaranteed. Include it in your dossier from the start.

03
After signing your lease
Pay the annual fee

You only pay once you have signed a lease. The fee is approximately 4.5% of your annual rent. For a €750/month studio that is about €405/year. You do not pay anything during the application phase.

Option 02 — Free Government Alternative
Visale

Visale is a free guarantor service from Action Logement, the French government. It is a strong option but has more conditions — read carefully before relying on it.

How to apply for Visale

Go to visale.fr, create an account, and apply for a "visa" eligibility certificate. You need your VLS-TS student visa already issued, a valid student ID, and a French IBAN (or use a Wise virtual French IBAN). Once approved, give the certificate to your landlord before signing the lease — the landlord registers on the Visale platform to finalize the guarantee.

The key limitation

Because Visale requires your visa to already be issued, you cannot use it to secure housing before you arrive in France — which is often when you need housing proof for your visa application. For international students, Garantme is almost always the better first choice precisely because you can get it before the visa.

Building Your Full Rental Dossier
What landlords expect.

Every landlord will ask for a complete dossier. Having it ready before you start searching saves days. Assemble this before contacting any landlord.

  • Cover letter (lettre de motivation). One page in French: your name, what you study, where (ConceptCore Academy, Arcueil), your monthly budget, and your guarantor situation. Landlords love this — very few applicants include one.
  • Passport or national ID — a clear scan, front and back
  • Certificate of Enrollment from ConceptCore — or pre-enrollment letter if UAI is still pending
  • Garantme or Visale certificate — include this prominently, it often gets you the apartment
  • Sponsor's 3 last bank statements — clearly showing regular income and current balance
  • Sponsor's 3 last payslips
  • Proof of address of your sponsor — a recent utility bill in their name
  • Your last 3 bank statements — or a Studely/AVI blocked account statement if using one for visa purposes

Where to Search
Verified platforms only.
Do not use generic sites

Avoid Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, or any non-French platform for Paris rentals. The scam rate is extremely high. Use only the verified French platforms below.

Studapart
// Best for internationals

Partnered with schools across France. Landlords here expect international students and readily accept Garantme. Full English interface. Best starting point.

Adele.org
// Student residences directory

Lists all private student residences in France: Nexity Studea, YouFirst, Estudines, Arpej. Furnished studios with all bills included. Easiest to book from abroad.

La Carte des Colocs
// Flatshares (colocation)

The best platform for finding a room in a shared apartment. Excellent way to cut costs in half and meet people in Paris immediately.

Jinka
// Aggregator app

Mobile app that pulls listings from all major French agencies at once. Set instant alerts for your criteria so you see new listings the moment they appear.

SeLoger / PAP
// Full French market

The two largest French real estate platforms. More competition and more listings than anywhere else. SeLoger = agencies. PAP (Particulier à Particulier) = direct from owners.

CROUS
// Social housing waitlist

Government student housing at below-market prices. Apply as early as possible — waitlists are long. Requires your student enrollment. Not guaranteed but very cheap if you get it.

Hidden upfront costs

Beyond your first month's rent, budget for: security deposit (up to 2 months' rent for furnished apartments), agency fees if going through an agency (capped at 1 month's rent by law), and Garantme fee (~4.5% of annual rent). Expect to have 3 to 4 months of rent available when you sign.

CAF housing aid (APL) — claim this

All students with a valid VLS-TS visa are eligible for monthly housing aid (APL) from the French government. Apply on caf.fr once you have a signed lease and a French bank account. The amount depends on your rent and income — typically €50 to €200/month. Processing takes 2 to 3 months. You will be back-paid, but do not count on it for your first months. EU students are also eligible.

Step-by-Step Search Process
How to actually find one.
01
As early as possible — ideally 3 months before arrival
Get your Garantme certificate first

Before you contact a single landlord, get your Garantme certificate. Listings in Paris get dozens of applications within hours. Having the certificate in your dossier from the first message dramatically increases your chances.

02
Set up your search infrastructure
Activate Jinka alerts immediately

Install the Jinka app and set up instant notifications for your target area, budget, and type. Good apartments disappear in under 24 hours. Speed of response matters more than the quality of your message.

03
For students abroad — book a student residence first
Use a student residence as a landing pad

If you cannot visit Paris before your move, book a student residence (Studapart, Nexity Studea, YouFirst) for your first 1 to 3 months. This gives you a legitimate French address for your visa application, takes the pressure off finding a permanent place from abroad, and lets you visit apartments in person once you arrive.

Recommended student residences near campus
  • Nexity Studea — strong presence in 13th and 14th. Furnished studios. Apply at nexity.fr/etudiant
  • YouFirst Campus — accepts Garantme and Visale. Modern facilities.
  • Estudines (Réside Études) — multiple Paris locations. Online application available.
  • Arpej — social housing operator, below-market rates. Apply very early — waitlists are long.
04
Responding to listings
Send your full dossier in the first message

Do not ask to visit first and send documents later. Include your full dossier as a PDF attachment in your very first message. Write a short, direct cover note in French (even Google Translate is fine): who you are, why you are moving to Paris, and that you have a Garantme certificate. This is what separates your application from 30 others.

05
Signing
Read the lease before signing — check these

A standard French furnished lease (bail meublé) is for a minimum of 1 year, or 9 months for students. Make sure you check these points before signing.

Lease checklist
  • Rent amount is within the legal rent cap (encadrement des loyers) for Paris — check at encadrementdesloyers.fr
  • Security deposit does not exceed 2 months' rent for furnished apartments (1 month for unfurnished)
  • Notice period to leave is 1 month for furnished leases (students have this right by law)
  • Inventory (état des lieux) is done together at entry and exit — photograph everything
  • Charges (water, heating, building maintenance) are clearly listed separately
Scam Protection
Paris moves fast.
So do scammers.
The threat is real

International students are prime targets because they are searching from abroad, under time pressure, and unfamiliar with French rental norms. Every year, students lose deposits to fake listings. Follow these rules without exception.

The Golden Rules
Non-negotiable.
  • 🚫
    Never wire money via Western Union, MoneyGram, or Neosurf. Legitimate French landlords only accept standard SEPA bank transfers. Any request for these methods is a scam. No exceptions.
  • 🚫
    Never pay a deposit before signing the lease. Money transfers only happen after both parties have signed a valid rental contract. No contract signed = no money transferred.
  • 🚫
    Never accept a listing that seems too cheap. A furnished studio in a good Paris neighbourhood for €500/month does not exist. If it seems too good to be true, it is a scam.
  • 🚫
    Never send documents to "build a file" before a video visit. Scammers collect your passport and financial documents to sell or use for identity fraud. No video visit or in-person visit = no documents.
  • 🚫
    Never accept a "landlord abroad" story. "I am a missionary / diplomat / researcher overseas and I will send the keys by post" is a classic scam script. Real landlords are accessible and local.
Document Safety
Protect your ID.
Watermark your documents

When sharing your passport, ID, or financial documents with any landlord or platform you do not fully trust, add a watermark across them using Filigrane Facile (filigrane-facile.fr — free, fully online). Write something like "Strictly for rental application at [address] — [your name] — [date]". This prevents your documents from being reused for identity fraud.

When is it safe to share documents?
  • After a successful video visit or in-person visit of the apartment
  • Via verified platforms (Studapart, SeLoger, PAP, agency portals)
  • After checking the landlord's identity (ask to see their property deed or tax notice)
  • Never via WhatsApp or email to an unknown individual without prior verification
Red Flag Checklist
Stop and walk away if you see these.
  • Price is suspiciously low for the location and size. Verified against real listings on SeLoger or Jinka.
  • Landlord refuses to do a video call or cannot show you the apartment physically or via live video.
  • Photos are stock images or appear on Google Images reverse search at another address.
  • Communication is only by email, no phone number provided, or replies come at odd hours from a foreign time zone.
  • Requests payment via Western Union, MoneyGram, Neosurf, crypto, or gift cards.
  • Lease is not a standard French bail meublé or the contract template looks unusual or is in English only.
  • Landlord asks for deposit before signing anything.
If you think you've been scammed

Do not send any more money. Document everything — save all emails, messages, and bank transfer confirmations. Report to Cybermalveillance.gouv.fr (the French government cybercrime portal). Contact your bank immediately if you have transferred money — a recall may be possible within 24 hours for SEPA transfers. File a police report (dépôt de plainte) at your local commissariat.
