Interview prep · ~20 minutes, conversational

The advisor checks one thing: that your study project is real and coherent. Prepare a 4-beat narrative, 30–60 seconds each: (1) your background, (2) why concept art as a career, (3) why ConceptCore — full-time 3-year program, 35h/week on campus, industry mentors, English-taught, (4) your career plan, with 2–3 named studios.

Know your file cold. The advisor has your dossier open and checks consistency: program dates and hours, tuition (€11,500/year) and who pays it, your financial-proof numbers (€615/month minimum + your tuition plan).

If asked about the school's recognition: answer honestly and calmly. The diploma is a school-issued diploma; concept art studios hire on portfolio, and you chose an intensive full-time program with industry mentors to build it. Don't raise the topic yourself. If a shorter stay or a visitor visa is suggested, point out the program is full-time, 35 hours/week, 3 years — the student visa is the correct category.

Bring: convocation, passport, acceptance letter, payment receipt, financial-proof documents, and your portfolio (tablet or prints — rarely requested, but for an art school it's the strongest proof your project is real). Arrive 15 minutes early. French or English both fine. Short, concrete answers. Never invent — the advisor's notes go to the consulate with your dossier.

Students from high-scrutiny countries (India, Morocco, Senegal, Vietnam) should be especially thorough. After your interview, send us the questions you were asked (academy@conceptcore.co) — it helps us prep the next students.

Heads-up · UI wording changes

The EEF interface is updated periodically. The buttons and section labels on the form (sections 1/2/3 above) are what we see today. The stable navigation path is Mon projet d'études → Je suis accepté → Acceptation papier reçue en dehors de l'application études en France. If you get lost, screenshot the page and email us at academy@conceptcore.co.

If you hit "Erreur technique" on upload or "To consult"

If multiple files trigger the error, the cause is almost always the 500 KB per file size limit (kilobytes, not megabytes). PDFs from typical sources are 1–3 MB and silently break the viewer. Compress each one (ilovepdf.com → "Compress PDF" → strong compression) until it is under 500 KB, delete the oversize version from "My supporting documents", re-upload the compressed one, click "To consult" again.

If only one specific file (often the Certificate of Enrollment) triggers the error, size is fine but EEF's viewer is rejecting that particular file. Try: (1) delete the broken entry, (2) redownload a fresh copy of the file from your email, (3) rename it to something simple like certificate.pdf, (4) re-upload. If the PDF still fails, open it, screenshot the full page, save as certificate.png, and upload the PNG instead.

If "Consult" still won't work, you can still submit. The "Consult" button is your preview, the Campus France reviewer does not use it. As long as the file appears in your "My supporting documents" list, it is part of your dossier. Submitting works even if Consult fails.

Fallbacks: log out, clear cookies for pastel.diplomatie.gouv.fr, log back in, try a different browser. If nothing works, send the error reference number and the file to academy@conceptcore.co.

"Duration of stay" field → enter ONE YEAR (12 months)

On the France-Visas form, the "duration of stay / number of months" field decides your visa type. Even though the program is 3 years, enter one year (12 months), not more. The first-year student visa (VLS-TS) is capped at 12 months by design and covers Year 1 only. If you declare more than 12 months, the system routes you to a different visa that forces a residence-permit application at the préfecture within 2 months of arrival. Declaring 12 months gives you the VLS-TS, which you simply validate online within 3 months of landing. You renew for Years 2 and 3 from inside France, before the first visa expires. "One year" also matches your Certificate of Enrollment, which covers a single academic year.

After Graduation
Staying in France.
Your options.
The good news

The Passeport Talent visa is a very realistic outcome for concept art graduates entering the games and animation industry. It gives you 4 years of legal residency, the right to work, and a direct path toward permanent residency.

One thing to know upfront

You need to transition to a work authorization before your student permit expires. This is very doable — but you need to start building your industry relationships from Year 2, not Year 3.

Path 01 — Recommended
Passeport Talent
01
Understand if you qualify
Eligibility — Salarié Qualifié

The most common Passeport Talent category for our graduates. You need three things: a signed job offer, a qualifying salary, and a recognized qualification or experience.

Requirements
  • A job offer or signed contract from a French employer
  • Gross salary of at least 1.5× the SMIC (~€2,700/month gross as of 2025)
  • Either a diploma of 3+ years of higher education, or at least 5 years of documented professional experience
Key advantage over a standard work visa

Your employer does not need to prove they couldn't find an EU candidate. This removes the biggest administrative barrier for studios wanting to hire you.

02
Your employer handles this step
Employer files with DREETS

Your employer (the studio hiring you) submits a work authorization request to the DREETS (Direction régionale de l'économie, de l'emploi, du travail et des solidarités). This is the regional labor authority. The studio's HR team will know this process — it is standard for international hires.

What your employer provides
  • Your signed employment contract
  • A formal request for work authorization (demande d'autorisation de travail)
  • Proof that the role and salary meet the Passeport Talent criteria
03
If you are still outside France
Apply for the visa at your French consulate

Once the DREETS approves the work authorization, you apply for the VLS-TS Passeport Talent at the French consulate in your home country. If you are already in France on a student visa, go directly to Step 04.

Documents for the consulate
  • Valid passport
  • DREETS work authorization approval
  • Signed employment contract
  • Proof of qualification (diploma or professional experience)
  • Proof of accommodation in France
  • Passport photos + visa fee
04
If you are already in France on a student visa
Change your status at the préfecture (ANEF)

You do not need to leave France. Apply for a change of residence permit status online via the ANEF portal. Do this before your student visa expires — a récépissé (receipt) from the préfecture allows you to stay legally while your application is processed.

How to apply online
  • Go to administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr
  • Select "Changement de statut" (change of status)
  • Choose the Passeport Talent category
  • Upload all required documents
  • You receive a digital receipt immediately. This is your proof of legal stay while waiting
  • Processing: 1 to 3 months depending on the préfecture
Timing is critical

File your change of status application at minimum 2 months before your student permit expires. The récépissé is your safety net — but you need to apply while still in valid status.

05
After approval
Receive your 4-year Passeport Talent card

Your new residence permit is valid for 4 years. You can work for your sponsoring employer, change employer after 1 year (with a simple declaration), bring family members to France under a simplified procedure, and travel freely in the Schengen Area.

What comes next
  • After 5 years of legal residency in France, you can apply for a 10-year resident card
  • After 5 years, you may also be eligible for French naturalization depending on integration criteria
  • Your family members receive a Passeport Talent — famille permit, allowing them to work in France without additional authorization
Path 02 — For Freelancers
Passeport Talent — Profession Artistique et Culturelle

A different Passeport Talent sub-category, designed for artists, performers, and authors of artistic work. The salary threshold is half of Path 01, you are not tied to a single employer, and you can mix freelance contracts with salaried gigs. For most concept artists, this is the most realistic post-graduation route.

Written with Liza Gomza

This section was written with Liza Gomza, a Russian concept artist who secured her Passeport Talent through this exact route while based in France. The application notes below come from her direct experience going through the process.

01
Understand if you qualify
Eligibility — Artiste-Interprète or Author

You must work as an artist-performer or as an author of literary or artistic work. Concept art qualifies. The financial and contract requirements are the main hurdles.

Requirements
  • Monthly resources of at least 70% of the SMIC (~€1,276/month gross as of 2025)
  • At least 51% of your income must come from your artistic activity
  • If salaried: contracts totaling at least 3 months over a 12-month period with companies whose primary business is creating or exploiting intellectual property
  • At least some of your contracts must be connected to France (French studio, French publisher, French client)
  • For a 1-year permit you need to show 12 months of resources. Longer duration = stronger income file.
  • No diploma requirement
Compared to Path 01

Half the salary threshold, no employer sponsorship needed, no diploma rule, no labor-market test. The trade-off: you build the file yourself — there is no studio HR team carrying the application for you.

02
Strengthen your file before applying
Register as auto-entrepreneur + INPI

Liza recommends registering as a French auto-entrepreneur (micro-entrepreneur) before applying. The INPI registration certificate becomes a strong supporting document — it shows you are an actual structured artistic professional based in France, not just someone hoping to be one.

Why this helps
  • Demonstrates a France-anchored professional structure
  • Lets you invoice French and international clients legally
  • The INPI certificate attaches directly to your application file
  • Registration is free at inpi.fr and only takes a few days
03
Apply online
Submit via ANEF — Passeport Talent — Artiste

Go to administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr, create an account, and apply for a new long-stay permit. Select the category Passeport Talent — Artiste. The form can be saved as a draft and modified multiple times before final submission.

Timing
  • Submit no earlier than 4 months and no later than 2 months before your current visa expires
  • Applying after expiry is not possible — start early
  • If your visa expires during processing, the préfecture issues a prolongation paper that keeps you legal until they decide
  • If approved, you pay €350 in tax stamps (€50 stamp duty + €300 tax) before the card is issued
Documents to prepare
  • Valid passport (usually 15+ months remaining)
  • Current long-stay visa or residence permit
  • E-photo (Photomaton booth)
  • CV, portfolio, motivational letter — written in French. Introduce yourself, explain what your job actually is, link to your portfolio and social media. Explain why France specifically: community, life in France, career opportunities. Mention your ConceptCore education and attach the certificate.
  • Proof of artistic or cultural activity (publications, awards, teaching) — optional for concept art if you don't have these
  • Contracts, signed NDAs, offers, gigs — show everything you have. At least some must be connected to France.
  • Bank statements proving 70% SMIC monthly with at least 51% from artistic work
  • INPI certificate from your auto-entrepreneur registration
  • Proof of accommodation in France (housing contract or utility bill under 6 months old)
  • Signed commitment to respect the principles of the Republic
04
After approval
Receive a 1 to 4-year Passeport Talent card

Unlike Path 01 which is always 4 years, this sub-category is granted for 1 to 4 years depending on the financial resources you document. Stronger income, longer permit. The permit still grants the same long-term trajectory: 10-year resident card after 5 years of legal residency, naturalization possible.

Note from Liza

If you just started your career and have signed contracts but no completed payments yet, that's fine — they may ask for additional papers later. The file is visual: treat it like a portfolio review, make it well-structured and easy to read.

Disclaimer

This route is based on Liza's personal experience as a non-EU student already based in France. Outcomes depend on your file, your préfecture, and how strong your French anchor is. For complex cases, consult a French immigration lawyer specialising in talent visas.

Path 03 — Fallback
Standard Salarié visa

If neither Passeport Talent route works (no qualifying salary, no qualifying artistic file), a standard Salarié work visa is the fallback. It works — but requires more from your employer.

01
Employer-side requirement
Opposabilité de l'emploi

Your employer must formally demonstrate that they could not find a suitable EU/EEA/Swiss candidate for the role before hiring you. They do this by posting the job with France Travail (formerly Pôle Emploi) for at least 3 weeks. This is the main friction point.

02
Same as Passeport Talent process
DREETS authorization, then consulate or préfecture

The process mirrors the Passeport Talent route: DREETS authorization first, then apply at the consulate if outside France, or change status at the préfecture if already in France.

Key differences to know
  • Permit valid for 1 year only, must be renewed annually
  • You are tied to your sponsoring employer — changing jobs requires a new authorization
  • No minimum salary threshold, but the role must match your qualification level
  • Family reunification is possible but requires a separate, slower procedure
Path 04

Independent concept artists
