You're free to come

As an EU/EEA/Swiss citizen you have the right to live and study in France with no visa, no residence permit, and no administrative procedure. The UAI timeline above does not affect your enrollment.

Work rights

You may work up to 964 hours/year (~20h/week) in France with no additional permit. Valid national ID or passport is all you need.

Receive your Certificate of Enrollment

Once the Code UAI is active, we issue your Certificate of Enrollment (Attestation d'admission) immediately. It is dated and signed, carries the Code UAI, and is the document Campus France requires you to attach inside Études en France. Without it, no visa procedure can begin.

What's on the document
  • Exact title of the program and the diploma prepared
  • Type and year of entry
  • Course start date
  • Latest authorized arrival date (for late arrivals)
  • Explicit statement that the program is full-time (>20 hours/week), as required by law

Financial Proof · Most Common Rejection Reason
How much you need to show.
The installment trap

The consulate requires €615/month for living expenses. Because ConceptCore allows monthly tuition payments, you must also show you can cover your monthly instalment simultaneously. Showing only €615 will result in rejection.

Accepted proof methods
  • AVI / Studely / Garantme (strongly recommended) for India, Morocco, Senegal and other high-scrutiny countries. Deposit the full year into a blocked French account; €615 released monthly. Gold standard proof.
  • Personal bank statements. Consistent balance over 3+ months, not a recent lump deposit
  • Financial guarantor. Sponsor's 3 bank statements + 3 payslips + tax returns + signed commitment letter
  • Scholarship letter. Official documentation covering your costs
Accommodation Proof
Where are you living?
  • French lease (bail). A signed rental contract. Strongest option.
  • Student residence booking. Certificate from Nexity, CROUS, Student Factory, etc.
  • Hosting certificate. Host's handwritten signed letter + French ID copy + recent utility bill in their name
  • Paid Airbnb / hotel (30–90 days) — accepted with a cover letter explaining you will find permanent housing upon arrival

After Graduation
Staying in France.
Your options.
The good news

The Passeport Talent visa is a very realistic outcome for concept art graduates entering the games and animation industry. It gives you 4 years of legal residency, the right to work, and a direct path toward permanent residency.

One thing to know upfront

You need to transition to a work authorization before your student permit expires. This is very doable — but you need to start building your industry relationships from Year 2, not Year 3.

Path 01 — Recommended
Passeport Talent
01
Understand if you qualify
Eligibility — Salarié Qualifié

The most common Passeport Talent category for our graduates. You need three things: a signed job offer, a qualifying salary, and a recognized qualification or experience.

Requirements
  • A job offer or signed contract from a French employer
  • Gross salary of at least 1.5× the SMIC (~€2,700/month gross as of 2025)
  • Either a diploma of 3+ years of higher education, or at least 5 years of documented professional experience
Key advantage over a standard work visa

Your employer does not need to prove they couldn't find an EU candidate. This removes the biggest administrative barrier for studios wanting to hire you.

02
Your employer handles this step
Employer files with DREETS

Your employer (the studio hiring you) submits a work authorization request to the DREETS (Direction régionale de l'économie, de l'emploi, du travail et des solidarités). This is the regional labor authority. The studio's HR team will know this process — it is standard for international hires.

What your employer provides
  • Your signed employment contract
  • A formal request for work authorization (demande d'autorisation de travail)
  • Proof that the role and salary meet the Passeport Talent criteria
03
If you are still outside France
Apply for the visa at your French consulate

Once the DREETS approves the work authorization, you apply for the VLS-TS Passeport Talent at the French consulate in your home country. If you are already in France on a student visa, go directly to Step 04.

Documents for the consulate
  • Valid passport
  • DREETS work authorization approval
  • Signed employment contract
  • Proof of qualification (diploma or professional experience)
  • Proof of accommodation in France
  • Passport photos + visa fee
04
If you are already in France on a student visa
Change your status at the préfecture (ANEF)

You do not need to leave France. Apply for a change of residence permit status online via the ANEF portal. Do this before your student visa expires — a récépissé (receipt) from the préfecture allows you to stay legally while your application is processed.

How to apply online
  • Go to administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr
  • Select "Changement de statut" (change of status)
  • Choose the Passeport Talent category
  • Upload all required documents
  • You receive a digital receipt immediately. This is your proof of legal stay while waiting
  • Processing: 1 to 3 months depending on the préfecture
Timing is critical

File your change of status application at minimum 2 months before your student permit expires. The récépissé is your safety net — but you need to apply while still in valid status.

05
After approval
Receive your 4-year Passeport Talent card

Your new residence permit is valid for 4 years. You can work for your sponsoring employer, change employer after 1 year (with a simple declaration), bring family members to France under a simplified procedure, and travel freely in the Schengen Area.

What comes next
  • After 5 years of legal residency in France, you can apply for a 10-year resident card
  • After 5 years, you may also be eligible for French naturalization depending on integration criteria
  • Your family members receive a Passeport Talent — famille permit, allowing them to work in France without additional authorization
Path 02
Standard Salarié visa

If the Passeport Talent salary or qualification threshold is not met, a standard Salarié work visa is the fallback. It works — but requires more from your employer.

01
Employer-side requirement
Opposabilité de l'emploi

Your employer must formally demonstrate that they could not find a suitable EU/EEA/Swiss candidate for the role before hiring you. They do this by posting the job with France Travail (formerly Pôle Emploi) for at least 3 weeks. This is the main friction point.

02
Same as Passeport Talent process
DREETS authorization, then consulate or préfecture

The process mirrors the Passeport Talent route: DREETS authorization first, then apply at the consulate if outside France, or change status at the préfecture if already in France.

Key differences to know
  • Permit valid for 1 year only, must be renewed annually
  • You are tied to your sponsoring employer — changing jobs requires a new authorization
  • No minimum salary threshold, but the role must match your qualification level
  • Family reunification is possible but requires a separate, slower procedure
Path 03
Freelance
Independent concept artists

If you want to take contracts from multiple studios rather than joining one full-time, you can register as a micro-entrepreneur (auto-entrepreneur) in France. For visa purposes, you would apply for a Passeport Talent — Porteur de Projet. This requires a business plan and proof of projected income. Viable if you already have client relationships. Consult a French immigration lawyer for your specific situation.
