Visual reference · the full Step 02 flow
1 · Create your account
  • pastel.diplomatie.gouv.fr/etudesenfrance → "Je m'inscris" (top right), or start from your country's Campus France site (usa.campusfrance.org, inde.campusfrance.org…)
  • Sign in via "Je me connecte" — the rest lives in your dashboard
2 · Open the paper-acceptance form
  • "Mon projet d'études" → "Je suis accepté" → "Acceptation papier reçue en dehors de l'application études en France"
  • One form, three numbered sections + a motivations field
3 · Fill the three sections
  • Section 1 · My case — tick "Je suis dans un autre cas" and pick the paper-acceptance / outside-catalogue option
  • Section 2 · My program — search the catalogue first (we won't appear), then tick "Je n'ai pas trouvé ma formation dans le catalogue" and fill manually:
  • Description · "Concept Art Program at ConceptCore Academy. Full-time 3-year program in concept design for video games, film, and animation. Year 1."
  • Year of entry · your year · Level · Bac+N notation: Bac +1 for Year 1, Bac +2 for Year 2, Bachelor's degree +3 for Year 3
  • Field of study · Arts - Culture - Design - Fashion · Type of diploma · Formation d'école spécialisée (alphabetical list — scroll past the "D" block into "F")
  • Section 3 · My establishment — ConceptCore Academy, 23–25 Ave. Jeanne d'Arc, 94110 Arcueil
4 · Motivations · Save · Finalize
  • Write your motivations (background, why concept art, why ConceptCore, career plan), click "Enregistrer" and attach your Certificate of Enrollment
  • You land on "I am finalizing my procedure" — verify your main study project matches your certificate exactly (dates, full-time, address). Fix with "To modify"; never DELETE
  • Skip the "Add" section — it's only for a supplementary program (e.g. French course) taken before ours. If you truly have one, use the second radio button; never "exchange program"
  • Open "Supporting documents" and confirm every file renders (see the red box below if it errors)
5 · Submit — the step students miss most
  • Saving is not submitting: your dossier stays in draft until you click "Soumettre mon dossier" / "Submit my dossier" at the end of the finalization
  • Pay the Campus France fee — $460 in the USA, ~€80–130 in most countries; many offices won't review until it's paid
  • Pick the "Independent" / non-degree-granting category — never Study abroad, DAP/HDAP or Doctoral. US form: no apostrophes in names (O'Malley → OMalley)
  • Your status should flip from Brouillon to Soumis. Still draft after a few minutes? You missed the button
6 · Track your dossier
  • Seeing "already submitted" when re-editing = your dossier went through. That's the lock, not a bug
  • Track in "Students already accepted"; read messages in "Messagerie" (an "À consulter" badge = unread)
  • Sequence: Soumis → En cours d'examen → Convocation à l'entretien → Avis pédagogique favorable
  • Wait for the convocation — Campus France schedules the interview at their office. Don't contact the consulate; that's the visa stage, only after the favorable Avis
  • Tell us which Campus France office has your file — we confirm your admission to them directly (required for non-connected establishments)
  • No movement for 2–3 weeks? Message your local Campus France office — faster than the platform
Interview prep · ~20 min, conversational
  • They check one thing: your project is real. Prepare a 4-beat story: background → why concept art → why ConceptCore (full-time 3-year, 35h/week, industry mentors) → career plan with 2–3 named studios
  • Know your file cold: program dates, tuition (€11,500 Y1), who pays, your €615/month + tuition-plan numbers
  • If recognition comes up: the diploma is school-issued; studios hire on portfolio — that's why you chose an intensive program. If a visitor visa is suggested: full-time, 35h/week, 3 years = student visa is the right category
  • Bring: convocation, passport, certificate, payment receipt, financial proof, portfolio (tablet or prints). 15 min early. FR or EN. Never invent — their notes go to the consulate
  • Afterwards, send us the questions you were asked (academy@conceptcore.co) — it helps the next students
"Erreur technique" on upload or "To consult"
  • Several files fail → the 500 KB per-file limit. Compress (ilovepdf.com), delete the oversize upload, re-upload under 500 KB
  • Only one file fails (often the certificate) → delete the entry, re-download a fresh copy, rename it certificate.pdf, re-upload. Still failing? Screenshot the document and upload as PNG
  • Consult still broken? Submit anyway — it's only your preview; if the file is listed, it's in your dossier
  • Last resort: clear cookies, try another browser, or email the error reference to academy@conceptcore.co
Heads-up · UI wording changes

EEF updates its interface periodically. The stable path is Mon projet d'études → Je suis accepté → Acceptation papier reçue en dehors de l'application. Lost? Screenshot the page → academy@conceptcore.co.

France-Visas / VLS-TS Étudiant

With your EEF agreement in hand, apply at france-visas.gouv.fr for the VLS-TS "Étudiant" — a 1-year visa that doubles as your Year 1 residence permit. Don't delay. Not yet submitted? Use the latest certificate (with UAI). Already under review? Only send the new version if asked.

Documents required
  • Passport covering your full stay + passport photos (French standards) + fee ~€99
  • Certificate of Enrollment (the one Campus France validated) + EEF Agreement (if applicable)
  • Financial proof + accommodation proof — see the two sections below
  • Civil liability insurance for France
"Duration of stay" → enter 12 months
  • The program is 3 years, but enter one year — the VLS-TS is capped at 12 months and covers Year 1 only
  • Declaring more routes you to a different visa requiring a préfecture residence-permit application within 2 months of arrival
  • 12 months = simple online validation after landing; Years 2–3 are renewed from inside France

Act 02 · Financial Proof — #1 Rejection Reason
How much to show.
The installment trap

The consulate wants €615/month for living costs — but if you pay tuition monthly, you must also prove you can cover the instalment. Showing only €615 = rejection.

ScenarioMonthly instalmentMinimum proof
Year 1, monthly plan~€920 × 10~€1,535/mo
Year 1, paid upfront€0€615/mo
Years 2–3, monthly plan~€1,200 × 10~€1,815/mo
Years 2–3, paid upfront€0€615/mo
Accepted proof
  • AVI / Studely / Garantme — blocked French account releasing €615/month. Gold standard; strongly recommended for India, Morocco, Senegal and other high-scrutiny countries
  • Personal bank statements — consistent 3+ month balance, not a recent lump deposit
  • Guarantor file — sponsor's 3 statements + 3 payslips + tax return + signed commitment letter
  • Scholarship letter covering your costs

Track C · After Graduation
Staying in France.
The good news

The Passeport Talent is a realistic outcome for concept art graduates: up to 4 years of residency, full work rights, and a direct path to permanent residency.

One thing to know upfront

You must switch to a work authorization before your student permit expires — very doable, but start building industry relationships in Year 2, not Year 3.

Path 01 · Salaried Hire
Passeport Talent — Salarié Qualifié
01
Do you qualify?
Eligibility
  • A job offer or signed contract from a French employer
  • Gross salary ≥ 1.5× SMIC (~€2,700/month gross, 2025)
  • A 3+ year higher-education diploma or 5+ years documented experience
Key advantage

Your employer does not have to prove they couldn't find an EU candidate — the biggest administrative barrier, removed.

02
Employer-side
Studio files with DREETS

The hiring studio submits the work-authorization request (contract + role/salary proof) to the regional labor authority. Standard process for international hires — their HR knows it.

03
If outside France
Consulate application

After DREETS approval, apply for the VLS-TS Passeport Talent at your French consulate: passport, approval, contract, qualification proof, accommodation, photos + fee.

04
If already in France on a student visa
Change of status via ANEF

No need to leave France: administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr → "Changement de statut" → Passeport Talent. You get a digital récépissé immediately (legal stay while processing, 1–3 months).

Timing is critical

File at least 2 months before your student permit expires — the récépissé only protects you if you applied while still in valid status.

05
After approval
4-year card
  • Work for your sponsor; change employer after 1 year with a simple declaration; free Schengen travel; simplified family procedure (Passeport Talent — famille with work rights)
  • After 5 years of residency: 10-year resident card, and possible naturalization
Path 02 · Freelance Artists — Most Realistic Route
Passeport Talent — Profession Artistique

Half the salary threshold of Path 01, no single-employer tie, freelance + salaried mixable. For most concept artists, this is the one.

Written with Liza Gomza

This section was written with Liza Gomza, a Russian concept artist who secured her Passeport Talent through this exact route while in France. The notes below come from her application.

01
Do you qualify?
Eligibility — Artist / Author
  • Monthly resources ≥ 70% of SMIC (~€1,276 gross, 2025), with ≥51% of income from artistic activity
  • If salaried: contracts totaling 3+ months over 12 with IP-creating companies
  • Some contracts must be connected to France (French studio, publisher or client)
  • 1-year permit = 12 months of resources shown; stronger income = longer permit. No diploma requirement
Compared to Path 01

Half the threshold, no sponsorship, no labor-market test — but you build the file yourself; no studio HR carries it.

02
Before applying
Register auto-entrepreneur + INPI

Liza's advice: register as a French auto-entrepreneur first (free, inpi.fr, a few days). The INPI certificate anchors you as a structured professional in France, lets you invoice legally, and attaches straight into your file.

03
Apply online via ANEF
Passeport Talent — Artiste
  • Apply on ANEF between 4 and 2 months before your current permit expires (after expiry = impossible). Draft can be saved and edited before submission
  • If it expires mid-processing, the préfecture issues a prolongation paper. On approval you pay €350 in stamps
The file
  • Passport (15+ months left), current permit, e-photo
  • CV, portfolio and motivation letter in French — what you do, links, why France (community, career, life). Mention your ConceptCore education + attach the certificate
  • Every contract, NDA, offer and gig you have — some must be France-connected
  • Bank statements proving 70% SMIC with ≥51% artistic income + INPI certificate
  • Accommodation proof (contract or utility bill <6 months) + signed commitment to the principles of the Republic
Note from Liza

Signed contracts but no completed payments yet? Fine — they may ask for extra papers later. The file is visual: treat it like a portfolio review, well-structured and easy to read.

04
After approval
1 to 4-year card

Duration depends on the income you documented. Same long-term trajectory: 10-year resident card after 5 years, naturalization possible.

Path 03 · Fallback
Standard Salarié visa
If neither Passeport Talent route fits
  • Your employer must prove they couldn't find an EU candidate (job posted 3+ weeks with France Travail) — the main friction
  • Then the same flow: DREETS → consulate or préfecture
  • 1-year permit, renewed annually, tied to the sponsoring employer; no salary threshold but the role must match your qualification; family reunification slower
Fully independent instead?

Register as micro-entrepreneur and apply for Passeport Talent — Porteur de Projet: business plan + projected income required. Viable with existing client relationships — get a lawyer's advice for your case.
